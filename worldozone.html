    Pammal Observes World Ozone Day

    Since 1995, on 16 September each year, the International Day for the Preservation of the Ozone Layer is celebrated. This year commemorates the 25th year of observance.  This date has been designated by the United Nations General Assembly in its resolution 49/114, to commemorate the signing of the Montreal Protocol on Substances that Deplete the Ozone Layer. This commemoration around the world offers an opportunity to focus attention and action at the global, regional and national levels on the protection of the ozone layer.

    In connection to this, the Pammal Magalir Federation by the invitation of the director of the Department of Environment Dr.H. Malleshappa, I.F.S organized an event and an awareness rally at Pammal, Chennai.

    The director of the Department of Environment for state Dr. H. Malleshappa, IFS., the State coordinator, National Green corps, Department of Environment, Mr. L. Durai, Er. Kirubakaran from the Tamilnadu State Pollution Control Board, the Chairman, Pammal Municipality Mr. C.V.Elangovan, attended the event and graced the occasion.

    Earlier in the day, the dignitaries visited the model biogas plant and were briefed about its functions after which the programme was started by planting trees near the model Bio gas plant, where the entire programme was conducted.

    The Pammal Magalir Federation’s head Ms. Mangalam Balasubramanian welcomed the dignitaries for the programme and gave an overview of the World Ozone day.  In his inaugural speech, the Chairman of Pammal Municipality Mr. C.V. Elangovan remembered the many programmes conducted by the Pammal Magalir Federation on Solid Waste management and other environment preserving activities done by the federation in the locality.  He congratulated their effort in bringing awareness to the locals by their programmes. 

    In his speech, Er. Kirubakaran from the Tamilnadu State Pollution Control Board gave an overview on what an ozone layer is and how it gets affected by the negligence of humans and the waste created by them.  He appealed to the participants of the rally to take the awareness of not using plastic bags and to go back to the good old days where there was little usage of plastics. In his speech, the state coordinator for the National Green Corps Mr. L. Durai, insisted on the importance of growing greeneries in the locality and appealed to create awareness on trees plantation. 

    In his Special address, the Director of the Department of the state for Environment, Dr. H. Malleshappa, I.F.S, congratulated the many good activities of the Pammal Magalir Federation on Environmental issues.  He wished to continue their good work and appealed to the members to continue to give patronage to this good cause.   Accepting the wishes, Ms. Mangalam Balasubramanian said that their motto is to think globally, act locally and do domestically and reminded the Department of the state for Environment on the proposal sent by them for planting trees on the Sengazhaneer Hill and mentioned the successful completion of the de-silting of the temple tank, which has increased the water table in the area to 15 feet from 80 feet. 

    After the programme, the awareness rally was flagged off by the Director, Dr. H. Malleshappa. Around 50 women from the self help groups in Pammal joined together in the awareness rally chanting slogans to plant more trees, to not to use plastic products, to reduce the use of vehicles and to use cycles often, to do rainwater harvesting and not to cut trees. The women members gave away handbills containing awareness message to the locals and demonstrated by collecting waste product and materials on the road.
