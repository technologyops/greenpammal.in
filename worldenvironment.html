    World Environment Day Celebrations at Exnora Green Pammal along with Pammal Municipality – 5th June 2013

    On 5th June 2013, the world observed Environment Day.  The theme for this year’s World Environment Day celebrations was Think.Eat.Save. Think.Eat.Save is an anti-food waste and food loss campaign that encourages to reduce one’s foodprint. According to the UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), every year 1.3 billion tonnes of food is wasted. This is equivalent to the same amount produced in the whole of sub-Saharan Africa. At the same time, 1 in every 7 people in the world go to bed hungry and more than 20,000 children under the age of 5 die daily from hunger.

    In response to this, Pammal Municipality and Exnora Green Pammal jointly observed the World Environment Day 2013 at the Vishweshapuram Vermicompost Shed at Pammal Municipality on the 5th June 2013.  The Pammal municipality had extended invitation to the Honorable Minister of Animal Husbandry Thiru. T.K.M Chinnaiya, B.A., to head the event and the Honorable Minister of Environment Thiru. M.C. Sampath to dedicate the recycling and resource recovery section in the Vishveshapuram vermicompost shed to the service of public.  The Pammal Municipality Chairman, Thiru. C.V. Illangovan, B.A.B.L., and the Vice- chairman, Thiru. P. Venkatesan,B.A., lead the event, while Ms. Mangalam Balasubramanian, Managing Trustee, Exnora Green Pammal welcomed the gathering and highlighted on the partnership of Pammal Municipality and Exnora Green Pammal and the various recovery and recycling activities  at Vishwesapuram.

    In his speech, the Minister of Animal husbandry, Thiru. T.K.M. Chinnaiya lauded the Solid Waste Management programmes carried out at the Pammal Municipality and the upcycled products made from waste water pouch and carry bags and the Minister for Environment, Thiru. M.C. Sampath congratulated the team effort of Municipal Administration and Exnora Green Pammal and recommended that the solid waste management programme done currently in 16 wards by Exnora Green Pammal be extended to all the 21 wards in the municipality.  He also congratulated the team for bagging the second prize at the Tamilnadu Pollution Control Board award ceremony earlier in the day.  The municipal Councilor for the 1st Ward, Thiru. V. Jeganathan delivered the vote of thanks.

    Prior to the programme, the ministers opened the kalvettu at the entrance of the shed and the resource recovery centre for the service of the public. During the programme, the ministers released an audio on ‘Kuppai vandi varuthu – an appeal to public on door to door collection’

    Prior to attending the event at Vishwesapuram shed, the ministers along with the municipal officials and leaders visited the biogas plant at Shankar nagar and Ms. Mangalam explained them replica tag heuer on the process of biogas generation. Resource recovery products were showcased and explained to the dignitaries.

    Ms. Mangalam explaining about the biogas generation process to the ministers and leaders.

    Pammal Municipality and Exnora Green Pammal are jointly implementing solid waste management at Pammal over the past decade.  The waste from the Pammal municipality finally comes to the Vishwesapuram shed, where it is further segregated to recover resources from it. Recovering resources and sending appropriate items back to the main stream as recycled and up cycled items, reduces the burden of the landfill area.  Pammal Municipality and Exnora Green Pammal are determined to reduce the waste that ends up at the landfill area and have been practicing recycling and upcycling methods for many years using simple, innovative and replicable techniques.  Currently, through this Resource Recovery Centre dedication programme, the services are showcased and dedicated to the general public. On this day it is an appeal to the general public to reduce and reuse their food and material waste as much as possible.

    Products made from resource recovered from the waste are explained to the dignitaries

    In this Resource Recovery Centre, recycling items like plastic products and wrappers, glass, tin, rubber etc are segregated and sent to recycling unit as they have good scrap value. Less scrap value waste such as plastic covers are separated and the less soiled ones are sent for upcycling unit where they are transformed into utility products such as bags, files etc.  The other part of the plastic covers find their way to the plastic shredding machines, where they are shredded into bits and pieces and they are used along with Bitumen to lay roads in the municipality area cheap replica rolex itself.  Many other neighbouring municipalities come to purchase from the Vishwesapuram Shed.  Waste from the coconut tree, the coconut leaves and shells find their utility in the industrial boilers and ordinary tea shops as they are made into briquettes and computer sambrani using the carbonization technique.  Organic waste from the wards are used to make organic manure rich in soil nutrients and sold in the name of “EXORCO”.  Finally the food waste, find their way to the community biogas plant, where energy is produced to light the streets and the same energy is used to light the cooking burners in the kitchens.  Thus maximum amount of waste is recycled, reused and reduced from the Pammal municipality side.
